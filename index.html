Mission Trajectory

The Vella Lab, part of the Division of Infectious Diseases at the Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia, seeks to advance human immune health through clinical and translational immunology. Our work focuses on understanding the diversity of human CD4+ T cell states, particularly T follicular helper (Tfh) cells, which coordinate antibody responses during vaccination, infection, and immune reconstitution.

Working directly with human clinical cohorts, we investigate how T cell programs are established and dysregulated in settings including infection, transplantation, vaccination, and inflammatory diseases such as graft-versus-host disease (GvHD) and MIS-C. We also study the mechanisms regulating Tfh differentiation and germinal center biology, translating these insights into strategies to improve immune-mediated therapies.

Through the development of innovative single-cell technologies and computational approaches, we link viral or therapeutic gene integration with cellular phenotype to uncover mechanisms of HIV persistence, determinants of CAR-T cell function, and the effects of gene modification on immune cell therapies.

By integrating clinical research, human immunology, and technological innovation, our mission is to advance precision immunology to uncover the cellular mechanisms that shape protective immunity and inform next-generation immune therapies.
